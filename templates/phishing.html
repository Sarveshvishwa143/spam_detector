Understanding Phishing Attacks

Your guide to staying safe online

What is Phishing?

Phishing is a type of cyber attack where criminals disguise themselves as trustworthy entities to trick individuals into sharing sensitive information, such as passwords, credit card numbers, or personal identification details. These attacks often occur through fraudulent emails, fake websites, or malicious links.

How Does Phishing Work?

  • Email Scams: Phishers send emails that appear to be from legitimate sources, such as banks or popular online services, asking recipients to provide confidential information.
  • Fake Websites: Attackers create fake websites that look like genuine ones, luring users into entering their login credentials or payment information.
  • Malicious Links: Phishing emails or messages often contain links that lead to fraudulent websites or initiate the download of malware.
  • Phone Calls and Text Messages: Attackers may also use phone calls or SMS messages to impersonate trusted organizations and trick individuals into providing sensitive data.

How to Protect Yourself from Phishing

  1. Be cautious with emails from unknown senders, especially if they contain urgent requests or suspicious links.
  2. Never share personal or financial information via email, phone, or text message.
  3. Verify the authenticity of websites before entering any sensitive information by checking the URL and looking for security indicators like "https" or a padlock icon.
  4. Use multi-factor authentication (MFA) to add an extra layer of security to your accounts.
  5. Keep your software and antivirus programs updated to protect against potential threats.
